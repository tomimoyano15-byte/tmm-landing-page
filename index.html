Homeowner's Guide

10 Things to Check Before Hiring a Painter

Most homeowners don't know what to look for. Use this checklist to avoid the painters who cut corners.

1

Are they QBCC Licensed?

In Queensland, any painting job over $3,300 requires a QBCC licence. No licence = no legal protection if something goes wrong.

2

Do they prep properly?

The biggest difference between a job that lasts and one that peels. Ask what percentage of the job is prep work. Below 40% is a red flag.

3

What paint brands do they use?

Dulux and Taubmans are industry standard in Australia. Ask for the exact product and finish. "Premium paint" means nothing without a brand name.

4

Can they show recent work?

Photos of completed projects, ideally on the Gold Coast. Bonus if they can connect you with a past client for a reference.

5

Do they carry proper insurance?

Public liability and workers compensation. Ask for certificates. If they can't provide them, walk away.

6

Will they give a written quote?

Verbal quotes are worthless. A professional painter will inspect your property, measure, and provide a detailed written quote — free of charge.

7

How do they handle furniture and floors?

Professional painters drop-sheet everything, mask edges properly, and leave your home cleaner than they found it. Ask about their process.

8

What's the warranty?

Quality painters offer a workmanship warranty. Check if the paint manufacturer warranty applies — it only does if an accredited painter applies it.

9

How quickly do they respond?

If a painter takes days to return your call, imagine how they'll handle issues during the job. Response time reveals reliability.

10

Are they Dulux Accredited?

Only painters who meet Dulux's quality standards earn accreditation. It means trained, tested, and held to a higher standard than most.
